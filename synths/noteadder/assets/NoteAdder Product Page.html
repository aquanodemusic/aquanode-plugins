What it does

One note in. A chord out.

NoteAdder is a MIDI plugin that processes incoming notes and adds harmony on top of them in real time. It produces no sound itself (when the sine wave audit mode is off) — it sits in front of your synthesizer and sends it a richer set of notes than you played. Everything stays in tune because it knows your scale. Then it animates those chords, records your output, and lets you customise every detail of how it looks and feels.

🎹

7 Note Slots

In manual mode, each slot adds a note at a chosen semitone and octave offset. Enable only the ones you need. Hit RND to randomise the whole set instantly.

🎼

16 Scales

From Major and Minor to Phrygian Dominant, Altered, Whole Tone, and Blues. Proper diatonic chord stacking for every scale degree, every mode.

🔄

7 Inversion Modes

Normal, Picker, Voice Leader, Drop 2, Cycle Up, Cycle Down, and Random Cycle. From static voicings to chords that evolve with every keypress.

✨

4 Animation Modes

Re-Voice, Wander, Drift, and Cloud keep held chords moving on their own. Available across all three plugin modes (Wander and Drift are Scale-only). Rate syncs to your DAW BPM or runs free at any millisecond interval you choose.

🎲

Humanize

Add up to 200 ms of random time offset and up to ±100 velocity spread to each added note independently. Instant feel, without a sequencer in sight.

⏺

MIDI Recorder

Capture the plugin's full output to a timestamped .mid file. Hit REC, play, stop — a save dialog appears ready to drop the file straight into your DAW.

🔒

Key Lock & Filter

Three options for out-of-scale notes: pass them through unchanged, discard them entirely for a hard key lock, or silently reroute them to the nearest in-scale note so you can play freely and always stay in key.

🎯

No Stuck Notes

All NoteOff events are tracked and matched precisely. Changing settings mid-hold, fast playing, and DAW stop all behave cleanly.

🎛️

Custom Chord Mapping

In Custom mode, assign any of 14 chord presets — Maj, Maj7, Maj9, Min, Min7, Min9, 6/9, Dom7, Sus2, Sus4, Power, Aug, Dim — or enter your own semitone offsets — to each of the 12 pitch classes. Every note on the keyboard can produce a completely different chord quality. Entirely independent of scale or key.

🎨

Per-Note Colours

Assign a custom colour to each of the 12 pitch classes. The interactive piano roll and scale colour key update live, with a full colour picker on every chip.

↕

Global Transpose

Shift every note out of NoteAdder by −12 to +12 semitones, applied after all other processing. Play a piece you know and hear it in a different key without changing your fingering.

Three modes

Choose how you want to work

Manual

Fixed Intervals

Define up to seven specific note offsets. Each slot has a semitone selector (−12 to +12 with full interval names) and an octave multiplier (−7 to +7). Hit RND to randomise all slots from a musical interval pool, or SAVE / LOAD to keep your favourite configurations as .nastate preset files. A Cloud animation mode adds floating counterpoint on top of whatever you've configured.

Scale

Diatonic Chords

Choose a root, a scale, and how many notes to add (1–7). NoteAdder stacks diatonic intervals from the played note upward — tertian by default, but you can switch to secundal, quartal, quintal, sextal, or septimal harmony. Playing D in C major gives you Dm7. Playing G gives you G7. Scale mode unlocks all five animation types including Wander and Drift, and shows a live colour-coded scale key you can click to repaint.

Custom

Per-Note Chord Mapping

Assign a specific chord type to each of the 12 pitch classes independently. Choose from 14 presets — Maj, Maj7, Maj9, Min, Min7, Min9, 6/9, Dom7, Sus2, Sus4, Power, Aug, Dim — or fully Custom, where you type your own comma-separated semitone offsets (e.g. -7,-3,5). You can also set any key to None to pass it through without adding anything. Every key on the keyboard can produce a completely different chord quality. Hit RND to generate a random mapping across all 12 notes, or RST to reset to clean defaults. Re-Voice and Cloud animation are both available in this mode.

Harmony stacking

Six ways to build a chord

In Scale mode, Harmony controls the interval step used when stacking added notes above the played note. Each produces a completely different chord quality from the same key and scale. Tertian is the western default, quartal is very nice too.

Tertian

Stack in thirds — the standard. Triads, sevenths, ninths.

Secundal

Stack in seconds. Dense, clustered, contemporary.

Quartal

Stack in fourths. Open, ambient territory.

Quintal

Stack in fifths. Spacious and parallel - power chord logic.

Sextal

Stack in sixths. Warm, inverted-third character.

Septimal

Stack in sevenths. Wide open voicings, sparse and airy.

Inversions

Voicings that move with the music

Inversion mode controls how the chord is arranged — which note sits at the bottom, how the voices are spread. Seven modes cover everything from static shapes to chords that reinvent themselves every time you touch the keyboard.

Normal

Notes stacked upward in natural diatonic order. Clean and predictable.

Inversion Picker

Choose root position, 1st, 2nd, or 3rd inversion by hand. Options limited to what your note count actually supports.

Voice Leader

Automatically picks the inversion that moves the least distance from the last chord. Progressions become smooth without effort.

Drop 2

The second-highest note drops an octave. Classic jazz voicing — tight chords open into something richer.

Cycle Up

Each keypress steps to the next inversion in ascending sequence. Repeating a note produces natural rhythmic variation.

Cycle Down

Same as Cycle Up, descending. Pair with a riff for harmonic movement that loops back on itself.

Random Cycle

Each keypress jumps to a random inversion. Structured unpredictability — harmonically correct, never quite the same.

Animation

Chords that keep moving

Hold a note and let the animation engine do the rest. Each mode transforms your static chord into something alive — re-voicing, wandering up the scale, drifting, or raining down atmospheric cloud notes. Rate syncs to your DAW BPM via standard subdivisions, or runs completely free at any millisecond interval. The Allow for longer notes toggle turns the rate from a fixed interval into a maximum, so some ticks stretch longer for a more organic, less metronomic feel.

Re-Voice

Cycles through every inversion of the held chord on each tick. Pads and long pads get harmonic movement without you doing a thing.

Scale Manual Custom

Wander

Each added note independently steps up or down the scale by a random amount within your set spread. The chord slowly drifts through the key.

Scale only

Drift

The entire chord root walks up the scale one degree per tick, carrying all voices with it. A slow upward harmonic journey from a single held note.

Scale only

Cloud

Fires a stream of short notes — density, octave spread, velocity range, and decay all customizable. Arpeggiated glitter, thick noise, or something in between.

Built-in tools

Tons of helpful functions, right there

🎹

Piano Roll Visualiser

A live strip along the left edge shows every active output note in your custom pitch-class colours. Click or drag to trigger notes directly — useful for auditioning voicings without a keyboard.

🔊

Audit Mode

The AUD button activates a built-in sine-wave preview of every currently active output note. Hear exactly what the plugin is outputting without routing to an instrument.

⏺

MIDI Recorder

Hit REC and play. The plugin captures all output events with precise timing against the host BPM. Stop recording and a save dialog lets you drop the .mid file straight into your project.

💾

Preset System

SAVE and LOAD buttons write and read .nastate files that store all manual slots, scale settings, animation parameters, inversion mode, and custom note colours in a single portable file.

🎨

Colour Customiser

Click any of the 12 pitch-class chips in the scale key to open a full colour picker. Colours flow through the piano roll and scale display. RST CLR resets to defaults; BW CLR switches to a clean black-and-white scheme.

↔

Resizable UI

Drag any corner to resize the plugin window. All text, controls, and the piano roll scale proportionally — from compact to large-screen at any ratio you need. Click the NoteAdder title twice to snap back to the default size instantly.

What you can do with it

A few starting points

1

Harmonised melody

Scale mode, Add = 1 or 2, Voice Leader on. Play a melody line and every note becomes a third or sixth, perfectly in key, moving smoothly.

2

Animated chord pad

Hold a note, Wander or Re-Voice on, BPM sync to 1/4. The chord reinvents itself every beat. Layers of movement from a single finger.

3

Glitter arpeggios

Cloud mode, density high, decay short, velocity ppp–mp. Every held note spawns a shower of quick soft notes — textural detail without a step sequencer.

4

Jazz comping

Dorian or Mixolydian, Add = 3, Drop 2 inversion, Lock bass on. Open voiced 7th chords with the played note anchoring the bottom.

5

Safe improvisation

Discard out-of-scale on, Add = 2. Play anything freely across the keyboard. Only in-key notes produce chords. No wrong notes possible.

6

Quartal jazz voicings

Scale mode, Harmony = Quartal, Add = 3. Every note produces a suspended, modern stack of fourths.

7

Power chords / octaves

Manual mode, +7 Perfect 5th and +1 Octave enabled. Every single note becomes a power chord, instantly, at any tempo.

8

Chord discovery

Manual mode, hit RND, toggle slots on and off. An unrepeatable way to find voicings you would never have reached by intention. Save what you like.

9

Per-key chord changes

Custom mode, assign Maj7 to C, Min9 to D, 6/9 to E. Each key on the keyboard triggers a completely different chord quality — no scale, no rules, total control.

10

Experimental intervals

Custom mode, set chord type to Custom on any pitch class, type in offsets like -5,2,8,13. Build clusters, quartal stacks, or intervals that have no standard name. Cloud animation on top for texture.
